Chemical structure of o-quinones

How is it formed?

In apples, enzyme polyphenol oxidase (PPO) can be found. When these apples are sliced, the PPOs react with oxygen, creating o-quinones.
O-quinones are the precursor to the trademark brown surface of old sliced apples. Quinones can generally be carcinogenic (have the ability to cause cancer) but the ones created by sliced apples are not significantly so.
